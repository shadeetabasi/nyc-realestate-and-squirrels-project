Mapping NYC Real Estate Data Against Street Tree Data

About Our Data Visualizations

It's no secret that New Yorkers are willing to pay more for tree-lined streets and living closer to green spaces, but will the data prove that?
Our team is looking to understand how the most expensive boroughs (by average sale price per unit) compare to the areas and boroughs with the greatest street tree density. Other questions we would like to answer include:
1. Are there specific species of trees in more expensive boroughs as opposed to more affordable areas?
2. Are trees in better condition (re: alive/dead) in areas with higher value real estate properties?
3. Is there a higher density of trees near specific property types or in boroughs with specific property types (e.g. condo, co-op vs rental walk-up, etc.)?

About the Project

As part of the Columbia University NYC Data Analytics Bootcamp Program (2020-2021), our team put together an analysis on NYC Real Estate Data measured against NYC Street Tree Data. Our project includes languages such as Python, HTML, CSS, Javascript and SQL. Libraries include Pandas, D3, JQuery, SQLAlchemy and more. Data visualizations constructed utilizing Plotly and Leaflet.
